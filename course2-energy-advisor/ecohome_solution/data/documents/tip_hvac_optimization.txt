HVAC Optimization Strategies for Smart Homes

1. Thermostat Scheduling
- Set thermostat to 68°F (20°C) in winter and 78°F (26°C) in summer during occupied hours
- Reduce heating/cooling by 7-10°F when away or sleeping to save up to 10% annually
- Use programmable or smart thermostats to automate temperature schedules
- Pre-cool or pre-heat during off-peak electricity hours before peak pricing begins

2. Peak Hour Avoidance
- Avoid running HVAC at full load during peak pricing hours (typically 6-9am and 5-9pm)
- Use thermal mass of the home: pre-cool to 74°F before peak hours, allow to drift to 78°F during peak
- In summer, run HVAC heavily during midday solar generation hours to offset grid cost

3. Maintenance for Efficiency
- Replace HVAC filters every 1-3 months; dirty filters increase energy use by 5-15%
- Clean evaporator and condenser coils annually
- Check refrigerant levels — low refrigerant forces the compressor to work harder
- Seal duct leaks; leaky ducts can waste 20-30% of conditioned air

4. Zoning and Ventilation
- Use zoned HVAC systems to heat/cool only occupied rooms
- Install ceiling fans to improve air circulation and reduce HVAC load by 4°C of perceived comfort
- Use whole-house ventilation during cool nights instead of AC when outdoor temperature drops below 20°C

5. Integration with Solar Generation
- Schedule HVAC intensive cycles (dehumidification, full cooling) during peak solar generation (10am-3pm)
- Use a smart thermostat that integrates with solar inverter data to optimize run times
- Battery-stored solar energy is best used for HVAC during evening peak pricing hours

6. Heat Pump Considerations
- Heat pumps are 2-4x more efficient than resistance heating
- Most efficient at temperatures above 5°C; below that, supplemental heat may be needed
- Run heat pump pre-heating in the morning using off-peak electricity before occupants wake
