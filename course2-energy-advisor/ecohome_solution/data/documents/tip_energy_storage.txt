Energy Storage Optimization for Smart Homes

1. Battery Sizing and Selection
- A typical home needs 10-15 kWh of storage to cover evening peak hours using solar energy
- Lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries offer the best cycle life (3000-6000 cycles) for daily cycling
- Size battery capacity to match your average evening consumption plus a 20% buffer
- Consider AC-coupled vs DC-coupled systems: DC-coupled is more efficient for solar-to-battery charging

2. Charge and Discharge Strategy
- Charge from solar first during peak generation hours (10am-3pm)
- If solar is insufficient, charge from grid during off-peak hours (10pm-6am) at lowest rates
- Discharge battery during evening peak pricing hours (5-9pm) to avoid high grid rates
- Set minimum state of charge (SOC) to 20% to preserve battery lifespan

3. State of Charge Management
- Avoid regularly charging to 100% or discharging below 10% — this degrades lithium battery cells faster
- For daily cycling, operate between 20% and 90% SOC to maximize battery lifespan
- Only charge to 100% when a grid outage is forecast or during extended cloudy periods
- Monitor battery temperature; performance degrades significantly below 5°C and above 40°C

4. Grid Interaction and Arbitrage
- Time-of-use arbitrage: charge battery from grid at $0.12/kWh off-peak, discharge at $0.22/kWh peak — saves $0.10/kWh
- Demand charge reduction: discharge battery to flatten peak demand spikes and reduce demand charges
- Enroll in virtual power plant (VPP) programs to earn payments for allowing the utility to dispatch your battery

5. Battery and Solar Coordination
- Set inverter to prioritize solar charging over grid charging at all times
- Configure export limits: if net metering rate is low, store excess solar in battery before exporting to grid
- Use weather forecast data to plan battery charge level: charge fully before multi-day cloudy periods
- On sunny days with full battery, shift more loads to daytime to avoid export waste

6. Backup Power Planning
- Reserve 30-50% battery capacity for backup if living in an area with frequent outages
- Critical loads (medical equipment, refrigerator, lighting) should be on a dedicated backup circuit
- Test backup functionality quarterly by simulating a grid outage in controlled conditions

7. Performance Monitoring and Maintenance
- Track round-trip efficiency monthly; healthy lithium batteries maintain 90-95% round-trip efficiency
- Monitor cycle count and capacity fade; most batteries retain 80% capacity after 2000-3000 cycles
- Keep battery management system (BMS) firmware updated for optimal performance and safety
- Inspect physical connections and ventilation annually to prevent thermal issues
