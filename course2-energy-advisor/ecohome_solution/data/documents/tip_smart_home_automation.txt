Smart Home Automation Tips for Energy Efficiency

1. Automated Scheduling Based on Occupancy
- Use motion sensors and door/window sensors to detect occupancy and adjust lighting and HVAC automatically
- Set devices to standby or off mode when no occupancy is detected for 15+ minutes
- Integrate occupancy data with smart thermostats to avoid heating or cooling empty rooms

2. Time-of-Use Automation
- Program smart plugs and smart appliances to run during off-peak electricity hours (10pm-6am)
- Automate dishwasher, washing machine, and dryer to start after 10pm using delayed start features
- Use smart outlets with energy monitoring to identify and schedule high-consumption devices

3. Smart Lighting
- Replace all bulbs with LED; LEDs use 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs
- Use dimmer switches and automated dimming schedules — reducing brightness by 50% cuts energy use proportionally
- Install daylight sensors to automatically turn off lights when natural light is sufficient
- Use motion-activated lighting in hallways, bathrooms, and garages

4. EV Charging Automation
- Schedule EV charging to begin during off-peak hours using the vehicle's built-in scheduler or a smart EVSE
- Integrate EV charging with solar production data: charge when solar generation exceeds home consumption
- Set a departure time so charging completes just before needed, minimizing idle energy drain

5. Smart Power Strips and Standby Management
- Use smart power strips that cut power to peripheral devices when a primary device (TV, computer) turns off
- Standby power (phantom load) accounts for 5-10% of home electricity use
- Monitor and schedule shutdown of entertainment systems, home offices, and gaming setups

6. Home Energy Management Systems (HEMS)
- A HEMS aggregates data from solar, battery, grid, and devices to make real-time optimization decisions
- Set priority rules: solar first, battery second, grid last
- Use demand response features to automatically reduce load when grid prices spike
- Connect smart appliances via protocols like Zigbee, Z-Wave, or Matter for unified control

7. Voice and App Control
- Use voice assistants to set energy modes (away, sleep, eco) across all connected devices at once
- Review weekly energy reports from your smart home app to identify consumption patterns
- Set budget alerts to get notified when daily energy cost exceeds a threshold
